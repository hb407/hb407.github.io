About Me

I am a postdoctoral research associate working with Prof. David Wales at the University of Cambridge, U.K. My primary research involves investigating the properties of electronic structure energy landscapes. In particular, I am interested in understanding the topology of multiple solutions to the self-consistent field equations. My other projects include exploiting nonorthogonal many-electron basis states for describing strong correlation in molecules, and constructing non-Hermitian analytic continuations that allow an electronic ground state to be smoothly morphed into an excited state.

I completed my PhD under the supervision of Dr Alex Thom at the University of Cambridge in 2020. During this period, I pioneered the holomorphic Hartree-Fock approach for generating continuous Hartree-Fock solutions as a basis for multireference calculations. In holomorphic Hartree-Fock theory, conventional Hartree-Fock solutions are analytically continued into the complex plane beyond the molecular structures at which they vanish. My thesis showed that construcing a linear combination of these solutions allows qualitatively accurate electronic energies to be predicted for bond-breaking processes and molecular binding curves.

In the summer of 2018, I undertook a two-month research internship at Q-Chem Inc., an electronic structure software package based in Pleasanton CA, USA. At Q-Chem, I developed a new dedicated library for running nonorthogonal configuration interaction calcuations on molecules. This library is now available in latest public release of the Q-Chem software package.

Prior to my PhD, I studied chemistry at the University of Cambridge. As part of my studies, I completed a research project with Prof. Daan Frenkel, investigating the thermophoretic flow of atoms under the influence of temperature graidents at liquid-liquid interfaces.
